Drawing a realistic sitting cat with visible fur shading is a complex task often beyond the capabilities of SVG graphics. SVG is more suited for simple, flat or semi-flat graphics. It lacks the ability to create detailed texture or very realistic images such as fur shading, which is generally the domain of raster graphics and digital painting software. However, here's a simplistic representation of a sitting cat:

```xml

  <circle cx="25" cy="25" r="15" stroke-width="1"/>
  <circle cx="20" cy="20" r="3" fill="black"/>
  <circle cx="30" cy="20" r="3" fill="black"/>
  <path d="M 25 30 Q 30 35, 25 40" stroke-width="1" fill="transparent"/>
  <path d="M 25 30 Q 20 35, 25 40" stroke-width="1" fill="transparent"/>
  <path d="M 10 25 Q 20 10, 25 25" stroke-width="1" fill="#000"/>
  <path d="M 40 25 Q 30 10, 25 25" stroke-width="1" fill="#000"/>
  <path d="M 15 35 Q 10 40, 25 50 Q 40 40, 35 35" stroke-width="1" fill="#000"/>
</svg>
```
This SVG creates a simple cat face with two black circles for eyes, and two paths for its mouth giving the look of a sitting cat. It also outlines the ears and body of the cat. Note that this is a very simplified representation and not a detailed or realistic image. For creating a highly realistic image with visible fur shading, you would typically use a conceptual art software like Adobe Photoshop or Illustrator.
